How to Replace a Faucet Cartridge

Best for: dripping faucet, stiff handle, poor temperature control.

Big win: Take a photo of the faucet and the old cartridge. Match by brand + shape for the correct replacement.

Tools & parts

  • Replacement cartridge (match brand/model)
  • Allen key / screwdriver (varies)
  • Adjustable wrench (sometimes)
  • Silicone faucet grease (optional)

Step-by-step

  1. Shut off water under the sink (hot and cold).
  2. Open faucet to relieve pressure.
  3. Remove handle (often a set screw under a cap).
  4. Remove retaining nut/clip holding the cartridge.
  5. Pull cartridge straight out (wiggle gently; avoid forcing).
  6. Clean the seat inside the faucet body.
  7. Install new cartridge in the same orientation (tabs must align).
  8. Reassemble clip/nut and handle.
  9. Turn water on slowly and test.
