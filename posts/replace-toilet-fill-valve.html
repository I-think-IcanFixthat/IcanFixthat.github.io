How to Replace a Toilet Fill Valve

Best for: tank keeps refilling, noisy hiss, slow fill, weak flush due to low water level.

Stop if: the shutoff valve won’t turn or leaks badly. You may need a plumber or to replace the shutoff first.

Tools & parts

  • Universal toilet fill valve kit
  • Adjustable wrench or channel-lock pliers
  • Small bucket/towel

Step-by-step

  1. Turn off water at the shutoff valve (clockwise).
  2. Flush and hold the lever down to drain most water from the tank.
  3. Disconnect supply line under the tank (catch drips).
  4. Remove old fill valve: loosen the lock nut under the tank and lift valve out.
  5. Install new fill valve: set height per kit instructions, tighten lock nut snugly.
  6. Attach refill tube to overflow tube clip (don’t shove tube down overflow).
  7. Reconnect supply line and turn water on slowly.
  8. Set water level: stop about 1" below top of overflow tube.
  9. Test 3 flushes and check for leaks.
