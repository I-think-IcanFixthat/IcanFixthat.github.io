How to Replace a Toilet Flapper

Best for: toilet runs randomly, tank slowly drains, you hear periodic refilling.

Before you buy: Most flappers are 2-inch. Some are 3-inch. Measure the drain opening at the bottom of the tank if unsure.

Tools & parts

  • Replacement flapper (2" or 3")
  • Paper towel / small sponge (optional)

Step-by-step

  1. Turn off water at the shutoff valve behind the toilet (clockwise).
  2. Flush once to empty most of the tank.
  3. Remove the old flapper: unhook the chain, then pop ears off the overflow tube pegs.
  4. Clean the seat (the rim the flapper seals against).
  5. Install the new flapper onto the overflow tube pegs.
  6. Reconnect chain: leave a little slack (~1/2").
  7. Turn water on and let tank fill.
  8. Test: flush 2–3 times. If it still runs, adjust chain or replace the fill valve.
